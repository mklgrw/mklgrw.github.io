Answers, before the call.

What does the configuration actually do?

We turn Anthropic's $20-per-seat Claude for Legal into a tool your paralegal can run without training: MCP connectors wired into Filevine, Westlaw, iManage, DocuSign, and Microsoft 365; written playbooks for the workflows your firm runs by hand; and a one-page SOP per playbook.

Who does the configuration work?

We do. We are a configuration shop, not a software reseller. Engagements are scoped, paid by the firm, and run by people who have wired MCP connectors into Filevine and Westlaw before. No procurement cycle, no six-figure statement of work.

How does this compare to Harvey AI?

Harvey is roughly $1,200 per seat per month and sells into AmLaw 200 firms. Claude for Legal is roughly $20 per seat per month, a 98 percent price collapse, and ships with native MCP connectors small firms can configure. Comparable reasoning. Different economics.

Do we need to switch case management platforms?

No. Claude reads from your existing Filevine, Clio, MyCase, or Litify through MCP connectors and writes back to the same matter. Nothing migrates. Your matter structure, billing codes, and retention policy stay where they are. We work inside Filevine, Clio, MyCase, or Litify. If you run something else, mention it on the audit, one custom connector is included in the standard sprint.

Does this connect to Filevine and Westlaw?

Yes. Both ship as native MCP connectors. Filevine surfaces matters, contacts, and custom field data; Westlaw surfaces citations, KeyCite results, and case content. We combine them into playbooks, a Filevine-sourced demand letter, cite-checked against Westlaw, formatted to your template.

How long does a configuration take?

Three to five focused days, spread across one to three weeks. Day one: audit and connectors. Days two and three: playbooks. Day four: paralegal training against the SOPs. Day five: buffer for the issues that surface during the first paid matter.

What does the configuration cost?

Configuration sprint: a fixed fee between $950 and $7,500, scoped during the free audit. The Anthropic license is separate (~$20 per attorney per month, billed by Anthropic). Ongoing retainer: $150 to $1,500 per month. 30-day cancellation. No lock-in.

How do we know LayerTWO can deliver?

We are pre-revenue and honest about it. First 5 firms get the configuration sprint at $950 fixed + a free audit + a documented case study credit. If we don't deliver to the scope agreed during the audit, you don't pay.

Is our data safe?

Anthropic's enterprise terms exclude your prompts and documents from model training, encrypt data in transit and at rest, and support zero-retention mode. MCP connectors respect folder-level permissions, including ethical walls. We document the access boundaries in the SOP we hand off.
