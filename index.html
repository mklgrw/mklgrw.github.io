The problem isn't the model. It's the configuration.

Claude for Legal is here, and at roughly $20 per seat per month it is finally priced for a 5-to-30-attorney boutique. But a license is not a workflow. Out of the box, Claude is a blank chat box — it does not know your Filevine matter structure, your Westlaw research habits, or how your paralegals draft a demand letter. The capability arrived; the configuration that turns it into billable-hour leverage did not.

For a firm with no internal IT, that gap is the whole problem. The work of wiring connectors and building playbooks lands on a managing partner who bills $400 an hour and has no time to learn an MCP connector — so the license sits unused, or worse, an associate pastes a hallucinated citation into a filing and the firm joins the sanctions count.

  • The 90-minute setup interview. Configuring Claude well means a structured interview about every workflow and tool — time a partner rarely has and a paralegal isn't positioned to lead.
  • Billable-hour culture blocks self-serve. Every hour spent fumbling with connector settings is an hour not billed. Self-configuration is technically possible and practically dead on arrival.
  • Hostile tool UIs. Wiring Claude into Filevine, Westlaw, iManage, and DocuSign means navigating admin panels and connector permissions that were never designed for a litigation paralegal.
  • Escalating sanction risk. Unconfigured, ungoverned AI use is exactly what produced 1,300+ sanctioned filings by April 2026. A configured system with cited sources is the difference.

What we configure

Five workstreams, scoped to your stack during the audit. Every one is named work with a named output — not a generic enablement deck.

Filevine workflow automation

We wire Claude's MCP connector into Filevine so it reads matter data, document folders, and case timelines directly. That turns intake-to-matter setup and status updates into one-step playbooks instead of paralegal copy-paste across screens.

Westlaw research + drafting integration

We connect Claude to Westlaw so research and citation checks run against real authority, not the model's memory. Your associates draft memos with sources that exist — the single most direct defense against the citation errors driving AI sanctions.

iManage, DocuSign + Microsoft 365 connectivity

We wire the document and signature side of your stack: iManage for document management, DocuSign for execution, and Microsoft 365 for the Word and Outlook surfaces your attorneys actually work in. Claude reaches only the matters you authorize.

Practice-specific playbooks

We build the repeatable workflows your firm runs daily — demand letters, deposition summaries, discovery review, and intake. Each playbook is configured to your templates and voice, so the first draft lands close to your final, not a generic starting point.

Paralegal training + documented SOPs

We train the paralegals who will run these workflows every day and leave behind written SOPs for each one. When a new hire starts or a workflow changes, your firm has documentation it owns — not tribal knowledge that walks out the door.
