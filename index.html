Each major leap in health has come from a better way to understand the human body.

Blood tests let us see inside. Wearables made it continuous.

But nutrition, the most influential input of all, is still stuck in the dark.

Trackers count steps, calories, or hours of sleep, but they can’t tell you why you’re tired, foggy, or inflamed.

They can’t show you how food choices ripple into your energy, mood, recovery, or long-term risk.

The next leap isn’t just about better sensors or better models.

It’s about a new kind of system that understands behavior in context, and turns that understanding into interventions that actually work.

We think the future of health is whoever owns the food → behavior → outcome loop.

And right now, no one owns it.

LayerTWO is the intelligence layer for nutrition, behavior, and health.

Our system learns what you eat, when, why, and how much.

It models how those choices impact your biology, in real time, and then prescribes proven interventions to help you perform, feel, and live better.

It combines:

  • Closed-loop behavior feedback that links meals to biomarkers and symptoms.
  • Predictive modeling that anticipates fatigue, cravings, or poor sleep before they happen.
  • Research-backed protocols that adapt to your biology and lifestyle.

We’re not chasing generic advice.
